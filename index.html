Chapter I

Beginnings in 1780

1780 - 1852

On the slopes of Mutton Hill, in one of Westmoreland's earliest settlements, the farm was first laid out in 1780. The original home, summer kitchen, and barns rose from the land in that era‚Äîhumble, resilient, and tied to the earth.

A grand center chimney once anchored the home's heart, drawing warmth into its core.

Then, in 1852, a "renewal" reshaped its heart: the original chimney was removed and replaced with two newer ones, and a hand pump plus cast-iron sink were added to bring water from a dug well behind the house.

That same water system continues to function today, a living link to its past.

"Each generation leaves its mark upon this land."
